# Legal Concepts

Core legal rights and protections found in software licenses.

## Intellectual Property Rights

| Term | Plain Explanation | Where It Appears in Licenses |
|------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **Copyright** | The legal right of the creator to control how their work is used, copied, and distributed. A copyright notice identifies the copyright holder. | All licenses (copyright notice requirement) |
| **Patent Grant** | A promise in the license that the licensor will not sue the licensee for patent infringement related to the licensed software. Not all licenses include this. | Apache-2.0, GPL-3.0, MIT (limited) |
| **Patent Retaliation** | A clause that terminates the license if the recipient sues the licensor for patent infringement. | Apache-2.0, MPL-2.0, EPL-2.0 |
| **Patent Termination** | The automatic revocation of patent rights if certain conditions are violated, such as filing a patent lawsuit. | EPL-2.0, BSL-1.0 |
| **Trademark** | A symbol, name, or design that identifies a brand. Licenses typically do not grant trademark rights -- you cannot use the project's name/logo for promotion without permission. | All licenses (trademark exclusion) |

## Liability and Warranty

| Term | Plain Explanation | Where It Appears in Licenses |
|------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **Liability** | A limitation clause stating the licensor is not responsible for damages arising from use of the software. Almost all open-source licenses include this. | All open-source licenses |
| **Warranty** | A disclaimer that the software is provided "AS IS" without any guarantee of fitness for a particular purpose or merchantability. | All open-source licenses |
| **Disclaimer** | A statement that the tool's output is not legal advice and should not be relied upon for legal decisions. | LicenseWise reports |

## Attribution and Credit

| Term | Plain Explanation | Where It Appears in Licenses |
|------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **Attribution** | The requirement to give credit to the original author when using, modifying, or redistributing the software. | MIT, BSD, Apache, CC licenses |
| **Non-Endorsement** | A clause (like in BSD-3-Clause) that prohibits using the project's name to promote derived products without written permission. | BSD-3-Clause |
| **Include Copyright Notice** | The requirement to keep the original copyright notice intact when using or distributing the software. | All licenses with attribution |
| **Include License Text** | The requirement to include the full license text when distributing the software. | GPL, LGPL, Apache, MPL |

## Changes and Modifications

| Term | Plain Explanation | Where It Appears in Licenses |
|------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **Modifications** | Changes made to the original software. Licenses specify whether modifications are allowed and what conditions apply to them. | All licenses |
| **Document Changes** | The requirement to record and describe modifications made to the licensed software. | Apache-2.0, GPL, LGPL |
| **Derivative Works** | Any work based on or derived from the original software. Under copyright law, creating a derivative work requires permission from the copyright holder. | All copyleft licenses |

## Anti-Tivoization

| Term | Plain Explanation | Where It Appears in Licenses |
|------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| **Anti-Tivoization** | Provisions in GPL-3.0 that prevent hardware manufacturers from using technical restrictions to prevent users from running modified versions of the software on their devices. | GPL-3.0, LGPL-3.0 |
